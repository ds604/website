When $a \ne 0$, there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$ Similar to Laplace of Gaussian, the image is first smoothed by convolution with Gaussian kernel of certain width $\sigma_{1}$: \[ G_{\sigma_{1}} (x,y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_{1}^2}} e^{-\frac{x^2 + y^2}{2\sigma_{1}^2}} \] to get \[g_{1}(x,y) = G_{\sigma_{1}}(x,y) \ast f(x,y) \] With a different width $\sigma_2$, a second smoothed image can be obtained: \[ g_{2}(x,y) = G_{\sigma_{2}}(x,y) \ast f(x,y) \]
