Comments on Creativity

The entry appears more concerned with trying out the software library than with addressing the needs of the design challenge. The library is undoubtedly helpful, but creativity is constrained by trying to formulate the problem in terms of a pre-existing framework, and it's unclear that the framework is relevant to the goal of the challenge.


Review Criteria 2: Scale of the design (25%)
Overall Scale Score

Poor

Comments on Scale

The network represented is at the scale of a local cluster. It is not clear that the representation would scale up. The representation itself, as well as the struggle described to include different types of information, suggest that it would not. Additional comments are below.

Overall Effectiveness Score

Comments on Effectiveness

The sparklines are interesting, and a timeline metaphor could possibly be developed further. But there is no clear overview picture that can be derived from the display at a glance, speaking to what the primary concerns are, where they are, how are they related, where to look. The information that might give a sense of overall network health is not presented in a compelling manner.

Overall Submission

Marginal

Comments on Overall Submission

Comments on guiding metaphor: This design appears appropriate to serve the purpose of an interface for an analyst's drilldown workspace to organize their work on particuar segments once assigned tasks. But the dashboard paradigm of this sort does not serve the purpose of giving an overview of the state of the entire network in a compelling way that abstracts away underlying details and gives an immediate sense for its overall state of health. This does not appear to be visual design which would be appropriate for a large display. The design caters to the strengths of a particular software library, which, while undoubtedly strong, may not necessarily serve the needs of a large-scale display as described in the challenge. The design appears to be derived from real-world network analysis, appropriate for monitoring a cluster, but this is not at the scale described in the challenge. A text-centric display is appropriate for smaller networks and drill-down work, but this doesn't address the issues described in the challenge. The project description describes the challenges the groups faced, but in the design presented, these issues remain unresolved. Issues: "The heatmaps can accommodate very large amounts of regions, zones, and alerts as the network grows over time." Do the numbers 1 to 50 stand for individual machines? If so, this doesn't speak to the scale of the problem described in the challenge. If they are representing individual machines, this still doesn't speak to connections among them, or other issues relevant to analyzing a network as a whole. The described struggle to include cities beyond the U.S. highlights scaling issue that this design faces, and the fact that the scope of problem this design targets is not at the scale of that described in the challenge description. Geographic information doesn't have a representation other than the list of cities. Connectivity appears to have been considered, but didn't make it into the final design. This would all be information that an analyst would want, that doesn't receive representation in the design. General Comments: Time series is the guiding motif in terms of graphical display, and it appeared in the development that a timeline was emerging as a guiding idea. A timeline is certainly useful, but it's not so clear from the description how it would work. The one shown has a few dots on it, but it's not clear how they relate to the rest of the display. Age of issues is an important piece of information, but it's not clear how this guides analysis. The display doesn't suggest in any clear way what to look for, where to look, or how to interpret at a glance what is the overall health of the network. While this may be fine for the drill-down level where the analyst would be working, it doesn't address the primary concern of the challenge. It appears that formulating the problem in terms possible to address with the software library may have drawn attention away from the concerns of the design challenge.
